The sweat soaked locker room of a recreational hockey arena might seem like an unlikely birthplace for a punk rock band but for Calgary's Bring on the Storm that's exactly where it all started. Some post-game banter between drummer Clayton Fandrick and guitarist Brandyn Smith revealed there were some punk rock stars on the roster and the two quickly brought bassist Josh Wallace into the fold.

Together they began writing fast, technical and melodic songs reminiscent of bands such as Propagandhi, A Wilhelm Scream, and Strung Out while searching for a singer to round out the lineup. Cody Coates was enlisted after responding to an ad the group placed online and the fully formed unit set about recording a 4 song demo at Coates' home studio "Machete Landing." Armed with a short set list and an eagerness to hit the stage, the band played their first show on July 29th 2016, opening up for melodic skate punk pioneers Belvedere. Shortly thereafter, Coates expressed his desire to focus on other projects and the band enlisted the help of friend Chris Kreuger (formerly of Bird Attack Records alumni Torches to Triggers)

in early 2017. With the new lineup in place Bring on the Storm entered Echo Base Studios with Casey Lewis (Belvedere,The Evidence) and recorded 2 brand new singles to showcase their evolving sound. A spot on Heliocity Fest in Medicine Hat quickly catapulted the group back to the stage. Since then Bring on the Storm has been making a name for themselves by consistently sharing the stage with notable acts such as Daggermouth, Elder Abuse, Belvedere, Drive by Punch, Mystery Weekend, The Ativans and Old Wives. Currently the band is working hard to finish writing their first full length record and hope to release it in 2018. For fans of: This Is A Standoff, Propagandhi, The Fullblast, Strung Out, No Use For A Name
